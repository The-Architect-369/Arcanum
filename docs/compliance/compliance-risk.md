\# Compliance \& Risk Posture



> \*\*Not legal advice.\*\* This document describes a compliance posture and design intent for a user-owned, non-custodial system. Engage qualified counsel before launch and prior to jurisdictional expansion.



\## 1. Scope



This document covers:



\- custody and financial risk posture

\- privacy and data minimization posture

\- content responsibility posture

\- moderation and abuse posture

\- jurisdictional rollout posture

\- communications posture (how we talk about MANA)



This document does not replace:



\- local law

\- platform policies (app stores, providers)

\- sanctions regimes

\- legal counsel



\---



\## 2. Operating Principles (Design Posture)



\### 2.1 Non-custodial posture

Arcanum and ARCnet are designed to avoid centralized custody:



\- the system should not centrally hold user private keys

\- the system should not centrally hold user funds by default

\- any optional custody (if ever introduced) must be explicit, opt-in, and separately governed



\### 2.2 Data minimization

\- collect only what is required for reliability and safety

\- prefer local processing where feasible

\- avoid building personal dossiers

\- treat “proof” as different from “profiling”



\### 2.3 Transparency

\- protocol parameters, fees, and treasury flows must be publicly auditable

\- governance changes must be logged and reviewable

\- material changes to risk posture must be documented



\---



\## 3. Content Responsibility \& Platform Role



\### 3.1 User-generated content (UGC)

\- users are responsible for content they publish

\- users must comply with law and content boundaries



\### 3.2 Protocol posture

The system is designed as a decentralized protocol with community governance. It does not guarantee pre-screening of all UGC.



\### 3.3 Discovery vs. addressability (de-listing)

Where content addressing is used, community governance may:



\- \*\*de-list\*\* content from discovery surfaces

\- preserve referential integrity where required



De-listing is not a guarantee of erasure across the wider internet.



\---



\## 4. Privacy \& Data Protection



\### 4.1 Minimal collection

\- collect only what is required for core function

\- avoid collecting sensitive identifiers unless explicitly necessary



\### 4.2 User rights (GDPR/CCPA posture)

Where applicable and feasible:



\- provide consent-based controls

\- provide data export mechanisms

\- prefer user-controlled storage rather than centralized retention



\### 4.3 Selective disclosure

Where identity proofs are required, prefer:



\- attestations

\- privacy-preserving techniques (e.g., zk-friendly patterns) where feasible



\---



\## 5. Financial, Payments, and Token Communications



\### 5.1 Fiat on-ramps and KYC/AML

If fiat on-ramps exist, they should be provided by regulated third parties where required. The protocol should avoid acting as a custodial fiat intermediary.



\### 5.2 MANA positioning (communications constraint)

MANA is designed as:



\- a utility token

\- a permission/access unit

\- an infrastructure usage unit

\- a governance participation signal (multi-factor)



MANA is \*\*not\*\* marketed as:



\- an investment product

\- an equity substitute

\- a guarantee of profit

\- a dividend or revenue share instrument



Avoid statements that could imply profit expectation.



\### 5.3 Sanctions and restricted use

Where required by providers or law:



\- block interactions from sanctioned jurisdictions/entities

\- implement compliance controls at provider boundaries (on-ramps, app distribution)



\---



\## 6. Intellectual Property



\### 6.1 Creator rights

Creators retain rights to their works unless they choose to publish under alternative licenses.



\### 6.2 Documentation and code licensing

Licensing posture is defined in:



\- `license-and-attribution.md`



\### 6.3 Infringement handling

If notices are required by law or distribution platforms, the project may adopt a documented notice process that is consistent with governance and boundaries.

\## 7. Safety \& Abuse



\### 7.1 Prohibited conduct

Certain conduct is prohibited to preserve dignity and safety. See:



\- `dignity-content-boundaries.md`



\### 7.2 Reporting and redress

Where reporting channels exist:



\- response standards should be documented

\- actions should be auditable where possible

\- escalation should remain reviewable



\### 7.3 Minors

Where legally required:



\- age-appropriate controls may be required

\- restricted categories must be enforced strictly

\## 8. Jurisdictions and Rollout



\- launch should begin with a limited set of jurisdictions

\- expansion should be staged with counsel guidance

\- client UX may require localization and jurisdictional variation
