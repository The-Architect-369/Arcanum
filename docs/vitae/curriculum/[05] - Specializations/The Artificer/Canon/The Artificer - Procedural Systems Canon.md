## **I. Entry Procedure**

### **ARP-1 — Eligibility Verification**

Prior to activation, the system verifies:

* Core Vitae completion,  
* absence of unresolved technocratic, extractive, or opacity violations,  
* demonstrated respect for users, context, and maintenance responsibility.

Failure results in deferred entry.

---

### **ARP-2 — Orientation Disclosure**

On entry, the system presents:

* explicit non-claims (no authority, no mastery through tools),  
* risk disclosures (over-optimization, opacity, form dominance),  
* maintenance-as-ethics framing,  
* exit and reversibility assurances.

User acknowledgment is required.

---

## **II. Design & Construction Workflow**

### **ARP-3 — Design Intent Articulation**

The system invites articulation of:

* purpose and context of form,  
* affected users or environments,  
* anticipated maintenance obligations.

Design intent must be explicit and reviewable.

---

### **ARP-4 — Consent & Context Validation Gate**

Before construction, the system validates:

* situational appropriateness,  
* user or stakeholder consent,  
* alignment with ecological and ethical constraints.

Construction cannot proceed without validation.

### **ARP-5 — Transparent Construction Execution**

The system supports building by:

* enforcing legibility of structure,  
* surfacing tradeoffs and limitations,  
* preventing hidden control mechanisms.

---

## **III. Testing, Maintenance & Repair Workflow**

### **ARP-6 — Usability & Impact Observation**

The system monitors:

* user interaction,  
* unintended effects,  
* signs of opacity or coercion.

Findings are surfaced as observations, not judgments.

---

### **ARP-7 — Maintenance Cycle Enforcement**

The system requires:

* scheduled upkeep and review,  
* documentation of repair actions,  
* acknowledgment of decay or obsolescence.

Neglect triggers corrective prompts.

---

### **ARP-8 — Repair & Revision Gate**

If dysfunction or harm is detected:

* repair is prioritized over expansion,  
* revision or simplification is encouraged,  
* dismantling is framed as valid outcome.

---

## **IV. Safeguard Enforcement**

### **ARP-9 — Technocratic Drift Detection**

The system monitors for:

* authority claims through expertise,  
* exclusionary technical language,  
* dismissal of user feedback.

Detection triggers humility framing and authority suppression.

---

### **ARP-10 — Over-Optimization Control**

If efficiency overrides care:

* care-first reframing is introduced,  
* construction or expansion is slowed.

---

### **ARP-11 — Opacity Control**

If systems become unintelligible:

* transparency prompts are enforced,  
* documentation and explanation are required,  
* unresolved opacity triggers stall.

---

**V. Cross-Specialization Interaction**

### **ARP-12 — Deference Protocols**

The Artificer system must defer:

* transformation sequencing to the **Alchemist**,  
* ecological balance to the **Druid**,  
* ethical coherence to the **Philosopher**,  
* distortion checks to the **Illusionist**,  
* threshold pacing to the **Hierophant**.

---

### **ARP-13 — Dialogical Interface**

All Artificer outputs must be framed as:

* design rationales,  
* observations,  
* maintenance reminders.

Commands, prescriptions, or superiority claims are prohibited.

---

## **VI. Audit & Logging**

### **ARP-14 — Event Logging**

The system logs:

* design intents,  
* consent validations,  
* construction events,  
* maintenance and repair cycles,  
* revisions and dismantling actions,  
* exits and pauses.

Logs are immutable and auditable.

---

## **VII. Exit & Pause Procedures**

### **ARP-15 — Voluntary Exit**

Practitioners may pause or exit Artificer practice at any time.

The system ensures:

* clean disengagement,  
* no penalty,  
* no loss of standing.

---

## **VIII. Failure Handling**

### **ARP-16 — Protective Stall**

Upon repeated invariant violations, the system:

* stalls Artificer functions,  
* issues corrective guidance,  
* requires re-orientation prior to resumption.

---

## **IX. Canonical Assertion**

If Artificer procedures enable control, opacity, or technocratic authority, they are invalid.

The procedural system exists to ensure that form and tools remain humane, transparent, and in service to life.
