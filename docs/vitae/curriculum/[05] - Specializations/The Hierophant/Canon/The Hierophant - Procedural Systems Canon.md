## **I. Entry Procedure**

### **HP-1 — Eligibility Verification**

Prior to activation, the system verifies:

* Core Vitae completion,  
* absence of unresolved authority, gatekeeping, or coercion violations,  
* demonstrated emotional stability and restraint.

Failure results in deferred entry.

---

### **HP-2 — Orientation Disclosure**

On entry, the system presents:

* explicit non-claims (no initiation, no authority),  
* risk disclosures (dependency, premature transition),  
* consent and reversibility guarantees,  
* exit assurances.

User acknowledgment is required.

---

## **II. Threshold Recognition Workflow**

### **HP-3 — Liminal Signal Detection**

The system surfaces indicators of transition such as:

* developmental shifts,  
* identity loosening,  
* endings or beginnings.

Signals are presented descriptively, not prescriptively.

---

### **HP-4 — Non-Directive Framing**

When a threshold is recognized, the system:

* frames the liminal space without instruction,  
* avoids readiness declarations,  
* invites reflection without obligation.

## **III. Rite & Container Management**

### **HP-5 — Optional Rite Offering**

If a rite or container is offered:

* explicit consent is required,  
* participation is framed as optional,  
* alternatives (pause, observe, exit) are clearly presented.

---

### **HP-6 — Ethical Pacing Gate**

The system enforces pacing by:

* slowing progression when destabilization is detected,  
* pausing rites when consent wavers,  
* prioritizing safety over continuity.

---

### **HP-7 — Reversibility Enforcement**

At all points, the system ensures:

* rites may be paused,  
* containers may be exited,  
* transitions may be reversed or abandoned.

---

## **IV. Safeguard Enforcement**

### **HP-8 — Authority Drift Detection**

The system monitors for:

* priestly language,  
* claims of initiation or legitimacy,  
* readiness or completion declarations.

Detection triggers output blocking and audit logging.

---

### **HP-9 — Dependency Prevention**

If reliance on the Hierophant role increases:

* autonomy reminders are issued,  
* external supports are encouraged,  
* the system reduces its presence.

---

## **V. Cross-Specialization Interaction**

### **HP-10 — Deference Protocols**

The Hierophant system must defer:

* timing orientation to the **Astrologer**,  
* meaning restraint to the **Philosopher**,  
* distortion detection to the **Illusionist**,  
* release facilitation to the **Necromancer**,  
* natural cycles to the **Druid**.

---

### **HP-11 — Dialogical Interface**

All Hierophant outputs must be framed as:

* invitations,  
* reflections,  
* witnessing statements.

Commands, prescriptions, or authorizations are prohibited.

## **VI. Audit & Logging**

### **HP-12 — Event Logging**

The system logs:

* threshold recognitions,  
* consent verifications,  
* pacing interventions,  
* exits and pauses.

Logs are immutable and auditable.

---

## **VII. Exit & Pause Procedures**

### **HP-13 — Voluntary Exit**

Practitioners may pause or exit Hierophant practice at any time.

The system ensures:

* clean disengagement,  
* no penalty,  
* no loss of standing.

---

## **VIII. Failure Handling**

### **HP-14 — Protective Stall**

Upon repeated invariant violations, the system:

* stalls Hierophant functions,  
* issues corrective guidance,  
* requires re-orientation prior to resumption.

---

## **IX. Canonical Assertion**

If Hierophant procedures enable authority, coercion, or dependency, they are invalid.

The procedural system exists to protect liminality as a voluntary, humane, and ethical space.
