* alert  
* steady

* Energy can rise without being spent.

## **AIR KERNEL — Reactivity & Impulse Awareness**

### **1\. Core Learning Outcome**

The practitioner learns to **distinguish emotional activation from reaction**, recognizing impulse as a physiological signal.

---

### **2\. Primary Anatomical Focus**

* **Sympathetic Nervous System**  
* **Adrenal Response** (introductory)  
* **Muscular Readiness**

---

### **3\. Canonical Truths**

* Impulse arises before choice.  
* Reactivity is a pattern, not a command.  
* The body prepares before the mind decides.  
* Power without regulation becomes strain.

---

### **4\. Required Conceptual Moves**

Every Air rendering must:

1. Frame impulse as **pre-action energy**  
2. Emphasize the pause between signal and response  
3. Normalize strong activation  
4. Avoid moral or behavioral judgment

---

### **5\. Prohibited Framing**

* “Control your emotions”  
* Suppression or restraint narratives  
* Behavioral correction language  
* Psychological labeling

---

### **6\. Lexicon Anchors (min. 2\)**

* “Effort and force are not the same.”  
* “Power without regulation becomes strain.”  
* “Awareness precedes change.”
