* precise  
* unhurried

* Thought follows sensation.  
* Emotion informs cognition before awareness.  
* Signals travel faster than interpretation.

## **AIR KERNEL — Thought–Emotion Signaling**

### **1\. Core Learning Outcome**

The practitioner learns to **notice how thought arises in response to emotional sensation**, recognizing signaling loops without engaging them.

---

### **2\. Primary Anatomical Focus**

* **Central Nervous System**  
  * brain–spinal communication  
* **Interoceptive Signaling**  
* **Autonomic Feedback Loops**

---

### **3\. Canonical Truths**

* Thought is a response, not a cause.  
* Sensation informs cognition before language.  
* Loops stabilize when they are observed.  
* Attention shapes experience.

---

### **4\. Required Conceptual Moves**

Every Air rendering must:

1. Frame thought as **signal response**  
2. Emphasize sequence: sensation → emotion → thought  
3. Avoid naming mental content  
4. Normalize repetitive internal loops

---

### **5\. Prohibited Framing**

* Thought correction  
* Cognitive restructuring  
* Insight-seeking narratives  
* Interpretive explanation

---

### **6\. Lexicon Anchors (min. 2\)**

* “Attention shapes experience.”  
* “What is unseen still acts.”  
* “Stability emerges through repetition.”

---

**7\. Replayability Parameters**

May vary:

* metaphors of pathways or echoes  
* pacing of explanation  
* sensory emphasis (head, chest, gut)

Must remain invariant:

* sequence awareness  
* physiological grounding  
* non-analytical tone

---

### **8\. Completion Condition**

The practitioner demonstrates conceptual readiness to observe thought without engagement.

### **1\. Core Function**

---

### **2\. Primary Anatomical Focus**

* Brain–body signaling  
* Breath regulation  
* Postural grounding

---

### **3\. Canonical Truths**

* Thoughts move when attention is steady.  
* The body anchors awareness.  
* Presence reduces mental momentum.

---

### **4\. Required Conceptual Moves**

* Grounded posture or stillness  
* Breath allowed to flow naturally  
* Awareness maintained through mental activity

---

### **5\. Verification Semantics**

* Mental activity is valid  
* Presence is sufficient  
* Following thoughts without awareness is not

---

### **6\. Prohibited Framing**

* Meditation performance goals  
* Duration challenges

### **7\. Completion Condition**

The practitioner remains present while thoughts arise and pass.
