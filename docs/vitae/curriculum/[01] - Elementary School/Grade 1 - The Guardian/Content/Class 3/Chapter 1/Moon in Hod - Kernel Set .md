## **SPIRIT KERNEL — Thought Emergence**

### **1\. Core Function**

Establish a felt sense of **thought arising from sensation**, allowing mental activity to be observed as an echo rather than an origin.

---

### **2\. Required Tone**

* quiet  
* observational  
* neutral  
* receptive

---

### **3\. Canonical Truths**

* Thought follows sensation.  
* Perception precedes concept.  
* Mental activity arises before awareness.

---

### 

### 

### **4\. Prohibited Framing**

* Interpretation of thought content  
* Meaning-making narratives  
* Insight or realization framing  
* Identity association with thought

---

### **5\. Replayability Parameters**

May vary:

* imagery of echo, ripple, or reflection  
* pacing and spaciousness

Must remain invariant:

* observational neutrality  
* absence of instruction

---

### **6\. Completion Condition**

The practitioner remains present as thought arises without engaging or analyzing it.

---

## **AIR KERNEL — Sensory-to-Thought Awareness**

### **1\. Core Learning Outcome**

The practitioner learns to **notice how thought emerges from sensory input**, recognizing cognition as a secondary process.

---

### **2\. Primary Anatomical Focus**

* **Sensory–Cognitive Interface**  
* **Memory Activation Pathways**  
* **Cortical Pattern Recognition**

---

### **3\. Canonical Truths**

* Sensation informs thought before language.  
* Cognition reflects perception.  
* Thought is a response, not a cause.  
* Awareness precedes interpretation.

---

### **4\. Required Conceptual Moves**

Every Air rendering must:

1. Establish sensation → thought sequencing  
2. Emphasize observation over engagement  
3. Normalize spontaneous mental activity  
4. Avoid naming or categorizing thoughts

---

### **5\. Prohibited Framing**

* Cognitive correction  
* Belief analysis  
* Thought suppression  
* Psychological labeling

---

### **6\. Lexicon Anchors (min. 2\)**

* “Thought follows sensation.”  
* “What is unseen still acts.”  
* “Awareness precedes change.”

---

### **7\. Replayability Parameters**

May vary:

* metaphors of echo or reflection  
* pacing of explanation  
* sensory emphasis (sight, sound, touch)

Must remain invariant:

* non-interpretive framing  
* physiological grounding  
* neutral tone

---

### **8\. Completion Condition**

The practitioner demonstrates conceptual readiness to observe thought as signal.

---

## **FIRE KERNEL — Attentional Stillness**

### **1\. Core Function**

Activate **steady attention** in the presence of emerging thoughts without following or resisting them.

---

### **2\. Primary Focus**

* Attentional clarity  
* Non-reactivity to mental movement  
* Nervous system steadiness

---

### **3\. Canonical Truths**

* Clarity does not require silence.  
* Attention stabilizes cognition.  
* Thought loses urgency when witnessed.

---

**4\. Required Conceptual Moves**

* Invite noticing without following  
* Emphasize steadiness over control  
* Maintain relaxed alertness

---

### **5\. Prohibited Framing**

* “Clear the mind” instructions  
* Performance-based focus  
* Suppression metaphors

---

### **6\. Replayability Parameters**

May vary:

* attentional cues  
* pacing

Must remain invariant:

* non-fixation  
* calm presence

---

### **7\. Completion Condition**

The practitioner enters Earth with stable attention.

---

## **EARTH KERNEL — Observed Mental Activity**

### **1\. Core Function**

Experience **thought arising and passing** while remaining physically grounded and still.

---

### **2\. Primary Anatomical Focus**

* Brain–body signaling  
* Breath regulation  
* Postural grounding

---

### **3\. Canonical Truths**

* Thought moves when attention is steady.  
* The body anchors awareness.  
* Presence reduces cognitive momentum.

---

### **4\. Required Conceptual Moves**

* Grounded posture or stillness  
* Breath allowed to flow naturally  
* Awareness maintained through mental movement

---

### **5\. Verification Semantics**

* Mental activity is valid  
* Presence is sufficient  
* Following thoughts without awareness is not

---

### **6\. Prohibited Framing**

* Meditation performance goals  
* Duration challenges  
* “Empty mind” expectations

---

### **7\. Completion Condition**

The practitioner remains present while thoughts arise and pass.

---

## **WATER KERNEL — Reflection on Thought Emergence**

### **1\. Core Function**

Integrate awareness of how thought arises through reflection.

---

### **2\. Reflection Medium**

* Voice → text transcription  
* Stored for longitudinal reference

---

### **3\. Canonical Truths**

* Reflection reveals cognitive patterns.  
* Awareness weakens automatic identification.  
* Thought becomes informational when observed.

---

### **4\. Required Prompts (Adaptive)**

* What did you notice about how thoughts appeared?  
* Did thoughts feel connected to sensation?  
* What changed when you stayed present?

---

### **5\. Failure Semantics**

* Busy thinking does not block reflection  
* Absence of reflection blocks progression

---

### **6\. Replayability Parameters**

Reflection content is always unique.

---

### **7\. Completion Condition**

The practitioner offers sincere reflection on thought emergence.
