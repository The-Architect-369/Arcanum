## **AIR KERNEL — Abstraction, Ideas & Cognitive Capacity**

### **1\. Core Learning Outcome**

The practitioner learns to **recognize abstraction and idea expansion as cognitive processes**, distinguishing growth from overload.

---

### **2\. Primary Anatomical Focus**

* **Cognitive Processing Load**  
* **Working Memory Capacity**  
* **Attention Allocation Systems**

---

### **3\. Canonical Truths**

* Ideas accumulate before they integrate.  
* Abstraction increases mental demand.  
* Overthinking is a capacity signal.  
* Boundaries allow strength to endure.

---

### **4\. Required Conceptual Moves**

Every Air rendering must:

1. Frame abstraction as **expansion**, not insight  
2. Distinguish idea quantity from clarity  
3. Normalize cognitive saturation  
4. Emphasize pacing over accumulation

---

### **5\. Prohibited Framing**

* Intellectual superiority narratives  
* Encouragement of mental excess  
* Performance-based cognition  
* Interpretive explanation

---

### **6\. Lexicon Anchors (min. 2\)**

* “Boundaries allow strength to endure.”  
* “Structure is not restriction; it is support.”  
* “Stability emerges through repetition.”

---

### **7\. Replayability Parameters**

May vary:

* metaphors of space, volume, or layering  
* pacing of explanation

Must remain invariant:

* capacity-first framing  
* physiological grounding  
* non-judgmental tone

---

### **8\. Completion Condition**

The practitioner demonstrates conceptual clarity about capacity versus abstraction.
