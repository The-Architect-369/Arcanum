## **SPIRIT KERNEL — Mental Reactivity**

### **1\. Core Function**

Establish awareness of **mental activation and reactivity**, allowing cognitive energy to be felt without escalation, argument, or identification.

---

### **2\. Required Tone**

* alert  
* contained  
* neutral  
* precise

---

### **3\. Canonical Truths**

* Thought can activate without direction.  
* Mental urgency is not necessity.  
* Reactivity is optional.

---

### **4\. Prohibited Framing**

* Argumentation narratives  
* Mental dominance or “winning” frames  
* Problem-solving emphasis  
* Insight or realization framing

---

### **5\. Replayability Parameters**

May vary:

* imagery of ignition or motion  
* pacing and rhythmic tension

Must remain invariant:

* containment  
* neutrality toward activation

---

### **6\. Completion Condition**

The practitioner remains present with mental activation without engaging or accelerating it.

---

## **AIR KERNEL — Cognitive Reactivity & Urgency**

### **1\. Core Learning Outcome**

The practitioner learns to **recognize mental reactivity as a physiological activation**, distinct from reasoning or decision-making.

---

### **2\. Primary Anatomical Focus**

* **Cognitive Stress Response**  
* **Prefrontal–Limbic Interaction**  
* **Neural Activation Loops**

---

### **3\. Canonical Truths**

* Mental urgency precedes reasoning.  
* Reactivity accelerates cognition.  
* Effort and force are not the same.  
* Power without regulation becomes strain.

---

### **4\. Required Conceptual Moves**

Every Air rendering must:

1. Frame reactivity as **activation**, not intention  
2. Distinguish thinking from reacting mentally  
3. Emphasize the pause before engagement  
4. Normalize fast or forceful thinking

---

### **5\. Prohibited Framing**

* “Control your thoughts” language  
* Moral judgment of mental speed  
* Behavioral correction  
* Psychological diagnosis

---

### **6\. Lexicon Anchors (min. 2\)**

* “Effort and force are not the same.”  
* “Power without regulation becomes strain.”  
* “Awareness precedes change.”

---

### **7\. Replayability Parameters**

May vary:

* metaphors of ignition, pressure, or speed  
* pacing of explanation

Must remain invariant:

* physiological framing  
* neutrality toward reactivity  
* non-judgmental tone

---

### **8\. Completion Condition**

The practitioner demonstrates conceptual readiness to witness mental urgency without engagement.

---
