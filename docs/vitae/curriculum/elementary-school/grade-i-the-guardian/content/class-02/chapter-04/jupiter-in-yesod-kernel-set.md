## **AIR KERNEL — Emotional Capacity & Overwhelm**

### **1\. Core Learning Outcome**

The practitioner learns to **recognize the difference between emotional expansion and overwhelm**, understanding capacity as a physiological limit.

---

**2\. Primary Anatomical Focus**

* **Autonomic Nervous System**  
  * sympathetic saturation  
  * parasympathetic recovery  
* **Adrenal Response**  
* **Breath Depth & Rhythm**

---

### **3\. Canonical Truths**

* Emotional intensity increases before capacity does.  
* Overwhelm is a signal, not a failure.  
* Expansion without support leads to instability.  
* Boundaries allow strength to endure.

---

### **4\. Required Conceptual Moves**

Every Air rendering must:

1. Frame expansion as **physiological**, not psychological  
2. Distinguish capacity from intensity  
3. Normalize limits  
4. Emphasize pacing over growth

---

### **5\. Prohibited Framing**

* Emotional “breakthrough” language  
* Encouragement of overwhelm  
* Performance endurance metaphors  
* Comparative capacity framing

---

### **6\. Lexicon Anchors (min. 2\)**

* “Boundaries allow strength to endure.”  
* “Structure is not restriction; it is support.”  
* “Stability emerges through repetition.”

---

### **7\. Replayability Parameters**

May vary:

* metaphors of space, volume, or pressure  
* pacing of explanation

Must remain invariant:

* capacity-first framing  
* physiological grounding  
* non-judgmental tone

---

### **8\. Completion Condition**

The practitioner demonstrates conceptual clarity about capacity versus intensity.
