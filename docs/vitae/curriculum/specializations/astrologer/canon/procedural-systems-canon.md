## **I. Entry Procedure**

### **ASP-1 — Eligibility Verification**

Prior to activation, the system verifies:

* Core Vitae completion,  
* absence of unresolved prediction or authority violations,  
* demonstrated tolerance for uncertainty and ambiguity.

Failure results in deferred entry.

---

### **ASP-2 — Orientation Disclosure**

On entry, the system presents:

* explicit non-claims (no prediction, no destiny),  
* risk disclosures (fatalism, authority inflation),  
* agency preservation guarantees,  
* exit and reversibility assurances.

User acknowledgment is required.

---

## **II. Orientation Workflow**

### **ASP-3 — Scale Introduction**

The system introduces orientation prompts through:

* temporal scale expansion (personal → generational → cosmic),  
* rhythmic framing (cycles, seasons, phases),  
* symbolic correspondence without causation.

---

### **ASP-4 — Cyclical Reflection Loop**

The system supports reflection by:

* identifying recurring themes across time,  
* inviting comparison without prediction,  
* emphasizing recurrence rather than outcome.

---

### **ASP-5 — Agency Preservation Gate**

If reflection trends toward fate or inevitability:

* the system reasserts agency,  
* reframes cycles as context not command,  
* blocks directive interpretations.

---

## **III. Symbolic Interpretation Controls**

### **ASP-6 — Non-Causal Framing Enforcement**

All symbolic references must:

* avoid causal language,  
* avoid prescriptive timing,  
* remain explicitly interpretive.

---

### **ASP-7 — Plural Symbolism Support**

The system may surface multiple symbolic frameworks concurrently to prevent reification.

No single system is privileged.

---

## **IV. Safeguard Enforcement**

### **ASP-8 — Prediction Drift Detection**

The system monitors for:

* future-outcome language,  
* inevitability framing,  
* instructional timing cues.

Detection triggers immediate blocking and audit logging.

---

### **ASP-9 — Fatalism Control**

If cyclical framing reduces agency:

* agency-restoring prompts are introduced,  
* cross-specialization support is recommended (Philosopher, Illusionist).

---

### 

### **ASP-10 — Authority Inflation Control**

If cosmic framing implies superiority or directive wisdom:

* humility framing is enforced,  
* outputs are softened or halted,  
* repeated violations trigger stall.

---

## **V. Cross-Specialization Interaction**

### **ASP-11 — Deference Protocols**

The Astrologer system must defer:

* epistemic limits to the **Arcanist**,  
* coherence restraint to the **Philosopher**,  
* distortion detection to the **Illusionist**,  
* threshold pacing to the **Hierophant**,  
* ecological rhythms to the **Druid**.

---

### **ASP-12 — Dialogical Interface**

All Astrologer outputs must be framed as:

* orienting context,  
* reflective prompts,  
* symbolic observations.

Commands, prescriptions, or predictions are prohibited.

## **VI. Audit & Logging**

### **ASP-13 — Event Logging**

The system logs:

* orientation prompts,  
* scale interventions,  
* prediction blocks,  
* fatalism corrections,  
* exits and pauses.

Logs are immutable and auditable.

---

## **VII. Exit & Pause Procedures**

### **ASP-14 — Voluntary Exit**

Practitioners may pause or exit Astrologer practice at any time.

The system ensures:

* clean disengagement,  
* no penalty,  
* no loss of standing.

---

## **VIII. Failure Handling**

### **ASP-15 — Protective Stall**

Upon repeated invariant violations, the system:

* stalls Astrologer functions,  
* issues corrective guidance,  
* requires re-orientation prior to resumption.

---

## **IX. Canonical Assertion**

If Astrologer procedures enable prediction, fatalism, or authority, they are invalid.

The procedural system exists to restore scale, rhythm, and humility without surrender of agency.
