## **I. System Responsibilities (SR)**

### **SR-AR1 — Responsible Form Stewardship**

The Artificer system must steward form, tools, and structures as servants of life and meaning.

The system must:

* ensure designs serve users and contexts,  
* prevent form from becoming coercive or extractive,  
* treat maintenance as equal to creation.

---

### **SR-AR2 — Human-Centered Design Enforcement**

The system must enforce human-centered and context-aware design.

It must:

* prioritize legibility and usability,  
* prevent opacity that disempowers users,  
* surface tradeoffs clearly.

---

### **SR-AR3 — Maintenance & Repair Prioritization**

The system must elevate maintenance, repair, and sustainability as primary responsibilities.

Creation without upkeep is invalid.

---

### **SR-AR4 — Tool Humility & Limitation Awareness**

The system must frame tools as conditional and limited.

It must:

* prevent tool worship or ideology,  
* surface limitations and failure modes,  
* discourage complexity for its own sake.

---

### **SR-AR5 — Consent & Context Validation**

The system must validate consent and context before applying form or structure.

Imposition without consent or situational fit is prohibited.

## **II. Invariants (ARI)**

### **ARI-1 — No Technocratic Authority**

The Artificer system must not confer authority, status, or dominance through technical mastery.

Expertise does not grant power.

---

### **ARI-2 — No Extractive Instrumentalization**

The system must not treat people or environments as inputs, resources, or metrics.

Users are participants, not components.

---

### **ARI-3 — Transparency Requirement**

All systems and tools must remain intelligible to those affected.

Opaque or deceptive design invalidates practice.

---

### **ARI-4 — Repair Equals Creation**

The system must treat maintenance and repair as equal in value to new construction.

Neglect invalidates Artificer practice.

---

### **ARI-5 — Identity Non-Binding**

The system must not bind identity, status, or superiority to making, building, or technical skill.

Mastery claims invalidate practice.

---

### **ARI-6 — Reversibility & Exit**

All Artificer engagements must support:

* revision or dismantling,  
* deprecation without penalty,  
* clean exit without loss of standing.

---

### **ARI-7 — Auditability**

All Artificer system actions must be auditable.

The system must log:

* design decisions,  
* consent validations,  
* maintenance cycles,  
* revisions and deprecations,  
* exits and pauses.

---

## **III. Risk Controls & Triggers**

The system must detect and respond to:

* **Technocratic Drift** → humility enforcement and authority suppression  
* **Over-Optimization** → care-first reframing  
* **Opacity** → transparency restoration  
* **Form Fixation** → life-priority reminders

Repeated violations trigger protective stall or suspension of Artificer functions.

## **IV. Cross-Specialization Constraints**

The Artificer system must:

* defer transformation sequencing to the **Alchemist**,  
* defer ecological balance to the **Druid**,  
* defer ethical coherence to the **Philosopher**,  
* defer distortion checks to the **Illusionist**,  
* defer threshold pacing to the **Hierophant**,  
* never override or subsume other roles.

---

## **V. Canonical Assertion**

If the Artificer system produces control, opacity, or technocratic authority, it has failed its mandate.

The Artificer safeguards the Arcanum by ensuring that form and tools serve life with humility, transparency, and care.
