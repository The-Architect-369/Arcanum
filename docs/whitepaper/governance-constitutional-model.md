# Governance & Constitutional Model

## I. Governance Philosophy

ARCnet governance is not designed as pure majoritarian voting.

It is designed as **constitutional coordination**: a system of bounded change where core invariants remain stable while parameters and policies evolve through transparent process.

Governance exists to:

- maintain system integrity
- allocate treasury resources
- adjust bounded parameters
- approve protocol upgrades
- protect dignity boundaries

It does not exist to override core invariants.

---

## II. Constitutional Hierarchy

ARCnet governance operates within a defined hierarchy:

1. **Constitutional invariants (non-overridable)**
2. **Protocol parameters (governance adjustable within bounds)**
3. **Treasury allocation decisions**
4. **Module-level governance**
5. **Application-level policies**

This hierarchy ensures stability at the core and flexibility at the edges.

---

## III. Constitutional Invariants

At minimum:

- identity cannot be duplicated
- time-based progression cannot be accelerated through payment
- minting cannot bypass protocol bounds
- treasury funds cannot be executed without governance process
- governance authority cannot be assigned purely through capital
- constitutional structure changes require elevated thresholds and time-locks

These invariants protect long-term coherence.

---

## IV. Governance Weighting Model (Multi-Factor)

Governance influence may be derived from:

- MANA stake
- identity longevity (ACC age)
- participation consistency
- Vitae thresholds (recognition milestones, not rank)
- delegation mechanics

This design reduces capture via capital alone.

---

## V. Governance Phases

Governance evolves over time:

### Phase 1: Genesis Stability
- stability guard active
- limited proposal surface
- parameter tuning only

### Phase 2: Observational Activation
- expanded proposal submission
- limited treasury routing
- governance metrics tested

### Phase 3: Structured Differentiation
- full proposal surface
- developer module proposals
- treasury expansion
- validator decentralization

### Phase 4: Circulation
- broad governance eligibility
- mature validator distribution
- ARCnet used beyond Arcanum

Decentralization is phased, not immediate.

---

## VI. Proposal Types (High Level)

Governance proposals may include:

- parameter adjustments
- treasury allocations
- module approvals
- validator onboarding rules
- emission parameter adjustments (bounded)
- IBC channel approvals
- protocol upgrade scheduling

Operational detail belongs in the governance specification.

---

## VII. Treasury Governance (High Level)

Treasury rules:

- all allocations require proposal approval
- execution follows time-locks where appropriate
- transparency is public and on-chain

Treasury may fund:

- infrastructure upgrades
- security audits
- developer grants
- ecosystem development

---

## VIII. Delegation & Representation

Delegation may include:

- stake delegation to validators
- governance delegation to representatives

Delegation does not override identity signals or constitutional constraints.

---

## IX. Governance Security

Governance security mechanisms include:

- proposal deposits (anti-spam)
- slashing penalties
- time-lock execution
- parameter bounds
- elevated thresholds for structural change

---

## X. Relationship to Application Governance

The Arcanum application may implement moderation and module-level rules, but application governance cannot override ARCnet invariants.

Chain > module > application.

---

## XI. Long-Term Objective

The long-term objective is a governance system that is:

- decentralized in validator distribution
- participation-weighted in authority
- transparent in treasury stewardship
- composable for developers
- structurally neutral (non-ideological)
