# Tokenomics

## I. Overview

**MANA** is the native utility token of ARCnet.

It functions as:

- a permission key
- an infrastructure access unit
- a governance participation signal (multi-factor)
- a treasury funding mechanism
- a coordination medium for developers

The protocol prioritizes **utility over speculative velocity**.

---

## II. Economic Philosophy

The MANA economy is designed around five principles:

### 1) Utility before exchange
MANA derives value from access, infrastructure usage, governance participation, and developer integration. Exchange exists, but utility is primary.

### 2) Time over capital
Authority cannot be purchased directly. Payment cannot accelerate time-based progression or recognition thresholds.

### 3) Earned and purchased MANA
Two acquisition paths exist:

- **Earned MANA** — protocol-defined distribution for participation and cadence adherence  
- **Purchased MANA** — acquired through exchange  

At protocol level, both are equivalent as assets. Governance authority remains multi-factor and cannot be reduced to capital alone.

### 4) Controlled issuance
Supply is governed by on-chain parameters:

- emission schedules
- governance-bounded adjustments
- treasury allocation logic
- constitutional constraints

### 5) Structural scarcity
Scarcity emerges from demand for utility and infrastructure use, not from manufactured restriction or coercive design.

---

## III. Genesis Economic Phase (Permission-First)

Genesis prioritizes:

- accessible participation
- generous exploration capacity
- minimal friction
- limited sinks
- stability calibration over revenue extraction

During Genesis:

- MANA is abundant enough to explore
- governance authority remains partially restricted
- validator incentives stabilize
- treasury posture is conservative

---

## IV. Core Utility Functions

MANA is used for:

1) **Access & permissions** — module activation, feature expansion, developer access  
2) **Governance participation** — proposal deposits, bounded parameter changes  
3) **Infrastructure usage** — storage anchoring, execution costs, network resources  
4) **Developer integration** — payments/permissions for external builders  
5) **Treasury routing** — stewardship funding, audits, grants, protocol upgrades  

---

## V. Token Sinks

Sinks are required for sustainability.

Primary sinks include:

- module activation costs
- governance proposal deposits
- infrastructure usage fees
- developer deployment costs
- treasury funding allocations

Sinks are designed to be utility-bound, not psychological extraction.

---

## VI. Governance Weighting (Multi-Factor)

Governance influence may include:

- MANA stake
- identity longevity (ACC age)
- participation consistency
- Vitae thresholds (recognition signals)

MANA alone does not determine authority.

---

## VII. Validator Incentives

Validator incentives include:

- block rewards
- transaction fees
- governance-approved treasury allocations

Slashing penalizes:

- double signing
- downtime
- malicious behavior

---

## VIII. Long-Term Sustainability

Sustainability is driven by:

- utility demand growth
- developer ecosystem integration
- infrastructure consumption
- governance participation
- disciplined treasury management

Revenue is derived from infrastructure usage, not attention extraction.

---

## IX. Regulatory Positioning (Non-Legal Summary)

MANA is designed as:

- utility token
- participation medium
- governance signal
- infrastructure access unit

It is not designed as:

- equity
- dividend-bearing asset
- profit guarantee vehicle

This document is not legal advice.

---

## X. Economic Trajectory

Phases (high level):

1) Genesis — permission-first access  
2) Awakening — controlled circulation  
3) Differentiation — sinks deepen with real utility  
4) Circulation — external developer demand increases  
5) Interoperability — cross-chain integration where appropriate  

---

## Conclusion

The MANA economy is designed to be:

- utility-first
- constitutionally bounded
- participation-weighted
- sustainable over time
