# Governance Identity Model

Status: canonical-draft
Phase: Pre-Genesis
Layer: Identity

## Purpose

Defines how identity participates in governance without becoming reputation, status, or political capital.

## Core Principle

Identity grants continuity.
Identity does not grant authority.

Authority requires separate recognition, review, and governance process.

## Governance Identity Requirements

A governance-capable identity should support:

- continuity
- uniqueness
- recovery
- privacy
- accountability through receipts

It should not require:

- public profiling
- reputation scoring
- popularity metrics
- economic dominance

## Governance Participation Layers

### Layer 1 — Identity

Provides continuity only.

### Layer 2 — Eligibility

Provides candidacy for responsibility.

### Layer 3 — Authority Envelope

Provides bounded permissions.

### Layer 4 — Governance Action

Provides proposal, review, vote, and execution pathways.

## Identity Queries

Future ARCnet identity queries may expose:

- active authority scopes
- governance receipts
- continuity status

Must not expose:

- private Vitae history
- personal journals
- internal recognition records

## Constitutional Constraint

No governance identity implementation may create:

- social ranking
- governance castes
- inherited authority
- purchasable authority
- permanent authority immune from review

## Canonical Closure

Identity enables participation.
Governance enables action.
Authority enables responsibility.
None of these redefine the being.
