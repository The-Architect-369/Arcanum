# Self-Amendment Boundaries

Status: canonical-draft
Phase: Pre-Genesis
Layer: Protocol

## Purpose

Defines what Arcanum and ARCnet may evolve, what requires governance, and what constitutional invariants must survive every future version.

## Core Principle

Arcanum is designed to evolve.

Evolution is not permission to abandon first principles.

The protocol may change.
The constitution may be refined.
The implementation may be replaced.
Certain invariants must remain protected.

## Three Classes of Change

### Class I — Local Change

Examples:
- themes
- layouts
- local agents
- local workflows

Governance required: No.

### Class II — Governed Change

Examples:
- app behavior
- treasury policy
- receipt schemas
- governance mechanics

Governance required: Yes.

### Class III — Constitutional Change

Examples:
- identity model
- authority doctrine
- succession doctrine
- dignity boundaries

Governance required: Elevated.
Extended review required.
Extended timelock required.

## Constitutional Invariants

### Identity Possesses Dignity

No future version may reduce a being to utility, score, ownership, or economic value.

### Authority Is Not Worth

Authority envelopes represent responsibility.
They do not represent human value.

### Vitae Is Not Rank

Vitae may prepare responsibility.
It may not become caste, prestige, or superiority.

### MANA Is Not Legitimacy

Economic participation may not become the sole source of governance legitimacy.

### Governance Does Not Own Beings

Governance coordinates action.
Governance does not possess sovereignty over participants.

### Nodes Do Not Possess Sovereignty

Nodes facilitate participation.
Identity remains the continuity-bearing layer.

### Receipts Witness Facts

Receipts may record actions.
Receipts may not define meaning, worth, or essence.

### Authority Must Remain Reviewable

No authority may become permanently immune from review.

### Succession Is Stewardship

Succession preserves continuity.
It does not transfer ownership.

## Amendment Safeguards

Constitutional proposals should require:

- sponsorship
- council review
- governance review
- elevated threshold
- extended timelock
- public receipts

## Forbidden Amendments

No amendment may:

- create inherited authority
- create purchasable authority
- create identity ownership
- create worth scores
- create permanent unreviewable rulers
- convert Vitae into ranking
- convert treasury into private ownership

## Evolution Test

Every major proposal should answer:

1. Does this preserve dignity?
2. Does this preserve identity continuity?
3. Does this preserve the distinction between worth and authority?
4. Does this preserve governance accountability?
5. Does this preserve reviewability?
6. Does this preserve sovereignty at the identity layer?

If not, the proposal should be blocked.

## Canonical Closure

Arcanum should evolve.
Arcanum should learn.
Arcanum should adapt.

But it should never forget what it exists to protect.
