# Tempus Earning Policy

## Purpose

This document defines how Tempus participation may be acknowledged economically without turning time into pressure.

Tempus offers rhythm.

It must not create obligation.

## Core principle

Participation may be recorded.

Non-participation is neutral.

Timing may provide context.

Timing must not judge the participant.

## Eligible Tempus actions

Tempus-related actions that may be eligible for factual receipts or non-transferable participation credit include:

- optional rite recording,
- optional reflection-window participation,
- public event creation,
- public event hosting,
- calendar coordination,
- contribution to public seasonal/cycle resources,
- verified community support within a Tempus event.

Eligibility must be explicit before the action.

## RITES-first posture

Tempus participation should prefer RITES or another non-transferable participation credit before liquid MANA.

This allows participation to be acknowledged without producing immediate liquid inflation or perfect-play pressure.

A safe flow:

```text
chosen Tempus action
  ↓
factual local or ARCnet receipt
  ↓
optional RITES credit
  ↓
bounded conversion program if governance activates one
  ↓
MANA only through governed budget
```

## Direct MANA posture

Direct earned MANA for Tempus participation is allowed only if:

- governance defines the program,
- emission is bounded,
- policy is public,
- non-participation is neutral,
- missed windows carry no penalty,
- there are no streak multipliers,
- there is no perfect-play framing,
- the reward does not imply readiness or worth.

## Event creation and utility fees

Tempus may use MANA for explicitly priced utility.

Allowed:

- event creation fees,
- anti-spam costs,
- public calendar publishing,
- infrastructure anchoring,
- optional calendar tools,
- creator/event hosting tools.

Forbidden:

- paying to reopen a missed window,
- paying to recover a streak,
- paying to make participation count as better,
- paying to bypass readiness,
- paying to buy Vitae recognition.

## Missed windows

A missed Tempus window must not create:

- shame,
- penalty,
- visible failure,
- loss of dignity,
- loss of basic access,
- economic debt,
- reduced worth,
- regression in Vitae.

The app may show that a window has closed.

The app must not frame closure as failure.

## No perfect-play economy

Tempus must not implement:

- annual perfect-play totals,
- daily streak maximization,
- compounding attendance rewards,
- celestial reward multipliers,
- hidden urgency bonuses,
- loss-based deadlines,
- timed scarcity that pressures behavior.

## Receipt model

Allowed factual receipt types may include:

- `tempus_action_recorded`,
- `tempus_event_created`,
- `tempus_event_hosted`,
- `tempus_public_window_published`,
- `tempus_participation_credit_issued`,
- `tempus_event_fee_paid`.

Receipts must not include:

- readiness scores,
- success/failure judgments,
- spiritual status,
- advancement claims,
- worth language,
- missed-window penalties.

## Vitae relationship

Tempus can provide context to Vitae.

Examples:

- action occurred during a named window,
- action occurred after a period of rest,
- practice cadence exists over time,
- review packet includes factual timestamps.

Tempus cannot advance Vitae.

Vitae recognition must remain retrospective and review-bound.

## UI language

Allowed language:

- “Window open.”
- “Window closed.”
- “Participation recorded.”
- “Event created.”
- “Optional credit available.”
- “No penalty for silence.”

Avoid language:

- “You missed your chance.”
- “Streak broken.”
- “Perfect play.”
- “Double reward window.”
- “Required cosmic action.”
- “Pay to recover.”

## Canonical closure

Tempus may invite.

Tempus may witness.

Tempus may coordinate.

Tempus must not pressure.

Time remains unbuyable.
