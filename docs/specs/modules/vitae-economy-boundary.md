# Vitae Economy Boundary

## Purpose

This document defines how economy may touch Vitae without corrupting Vitae.

Vitae is recognition after stabilization.

Economy may fund instruction, tools, infrastructure, and review operations.

Economy may not sell becoming.

## Core principle

You may pay for support.

You may not pay for recognition.

Vitae must remain:

- stability-first,
- non-comparative,
- non-coercive,
- non-monetized as recognition,
- non-accelerable by payment.

## Allowed paid surfaces

The following may be priced if costs are explicit and participation remains voluntary:

### Curriculum access

Structured learning materials, courses, guided paths, media, exercises, and published educational modules may be paid.

Payment grants access to material, not recognized advancement.

### Tools and infrastructure

Paid tools may include:

- private storage,
- export tools,
- advanced AI tutoring,
- media generation,
- study aids,
- encrypted backup,
- review packet drafting,
- accessibility enhancements where a free meaningful path remains.

### Mentor or reviewer labor

Human or agent-assisted review labor may be priced when clearly framed as process support.

Payment may cover time, coordination, and administrative cost.

Payment must not guarantee recognition.

### Cohorts and events

Cohorts, workshops, learning circles, or guided events may charge for coordination and access.

Participation must not imply superiority over non-participants.

### Certification processing costs

If future certification or authority review has real administrative costs, fees may cover processing.

Fees must be waivable or subsidizable where governance requires accessibility.

Payment must not guarantee outcome.

## Forbidden paid surfaces

Payment must not buy:

- Vitae grade recognition,
- authority envelopes,
- readiness declarations,
- mastery claims,
- public rank,
- status labels,
- governance stewardship,
- treasury stewardship,
- protocol stewardship,
- bypass of required review,
- acceleration of stabilization periods,
- erasure of review concerns,
- dignity or belonging.

## Free meaningful path

Vitae must preserve a meaningful free path.

Free participation should include:

- reading core public doctrine,
- local practice recording where technically feasible,
- private self-reflection,
- basic path exploration,
- visibility into what recognition requires,
- the ability to remain silent without penalty.

Paid offerings may enrich the journey.

They may not become the only path to dignity or basic participation.

## Review boundary

Recognition review may consider evidence.

It must not consider payment as evidence of readiness.

Payment records may confirm that a service was purchased.

Payment records must not become proof of becoming.

## Chain boundary

ARCnet may witness factual economic events, such as:

- course access purchased,
- review fee paid,
- review packet submitted,
- scholarship granted,
- mentor session scheduled,
- refund issued.

ARCnet must not witness semantic judgments, such as:

- user is ready,
- user is superior,
- user purchased grade,
- user deserves authority,
- user is spiritually advanced.

## MANA boundary

MANA may pay for Vitae-adjacent utilities.

MANA may not buy Vitae itself.

Allowed MANA uses:

- optional tools,
- storage/infrastructure,
- course materials,
- review processing,
- cohort access,
- export or proposal drafting.

Forbidden MANA uses:

- grade purchase,
- recognition purchase,
- readiness acceleration,
- authority purchase,
- bypass of review,
- public status purchase.

## App language

Allowed language:

- “Unlock course materials.”
- “Pay review processing fee.”
- “Purchase optional tutoring.”
- “Generate a review packet.”
- “Recognition requires review.”

Avoid language:

- “Buy Grade II.”
- “Pay to advance.”
- “Unlock mastery rank.”
- “Premium users progress faster.”
- “Purchase readiness.”

## Scholarships and accessibility

Governance may create scholarship, grant, or sponsored access programs.

Such programs should preserve dignity and avoid public poverty labels.

A scholarship is access support, not lower recognition.

## Canonical closure

The work may be supported.

The tools may be priced.

The path may be taught.

Recognition may not be sold.
