# MANA Governance Model

Status: canonical-draft
Phase: Pre-Genesis
Layer: Economy

## Purpose

Defines the relationship between MANA, governance, authority, treasury, identity, and ARCnet.

## Core Principle

MANA is an economic instrument.

MANA is not:
- identity
- authority
- Vitae
- governance legitimacy
- worth

## Constitutional Constraints

MANA may not:

- purchase authority
- purchase identity
- purchase Vitae recognition
- bypass governance review
- bypass treasury review

Economic participation must remain distinct from human value.

## MANA and Governance

MANA may contribute signal.

MANA may not become sole governance authority.

Governance should consider:

- identity continuity
- participation
- authority envelopes
- governance process

before economic influence.

## MANA and Treasury

Treasury actions may move MANA.

Treasury actions must require:

- proposal
- review
- governance authorization
- timelock
- execution receipt

## MANA and Authority

Authority may review MANA flows.

MANA must never create authority automatically.

Example:

Holding more MANA must not automatically create:

- governance steward status
- treasury steward status
- protocol steward status

## MANA and ARCnet

ARCnet may witness:

- treasury execution
- governed allocation
- authorized distribution

ARCnet should not infer social value from balances.

## Observation Model

The network may observe:

- treasury allocations
- governed distributions
- execution receipts

The network should avoid creating status hierarchies around holdings.

## Future Questions

Future governance activation must define:

- voting weight policy
- treasury allocation formulas
- issuance policy
- burn policy
- stewardship incentives

These decisions remain subordinate to doctrine.

## Canonical Closure

MANA enables economic coordination.
Governance enables collective decision making.
Identity enables continuity.

None of these should be allowed to replace the others.
